Features

Everything You Need on Stage

Built by a working musician, for working musicians. ShowTime handles the tech so you can focus on the music.

🔄

Master/Slave Sync

Connect your entire band wirelessly. When you change songs, transpose, or turn the page, every device updates instantly. No app install — band members just open a browser.

🎵

Instant Transpose

Transpose any chart up or down with one tap. Built-in Eb and Bb instrument modes so horn players and keyboard players always see their own key automatically.

📽️

Pre-Show & Intermission Slides

Load images or videos for your pre-show and intermission. Hit one button and they play on the projector automatically — complete with Ken Burns effects and transitions. Your roadie can run it from any device.

🎤

Open Mic & Backing Band

Run open mic nights with ease. Manage guest performers, track the running order, and provide backing charts to walk-up artists — all from the same system your band already uses.

🦶

Foot Pedal Navigation

Hands-free page turns and song navigation via Bluetooth foot pedals. Supports 3-pedal and 4-pedal controllers. Keep your hands on the instrument, not the tablet.

✏️

Annotations

Add personal notes, cues, and reminders directly on your charts — with instrument-specific colour coding. Drag them anywhere on the page and they stay put.

📱

Audience Song Requests

Generate a QR code your audience can scan to browse your song list and request songs. Perfect for pub residencies, functions, and open mic nights.

🎼

Multiple Chart Formats

HTML chord charts, PDF fakebook pages, and MusicXML sheet music all in one song. Switch between arrangements in one tap — jazz chart, simplified chart, or the full score.

🎹

Circle of Fifths

Interactive Circle of Fifths popup for quick key reference, chord relationships, and transposition guidance — right there on your performance screen when you need it.

How It Works

Up and Running in Minutes

1

Import Your Charts

Paste from Word, import RTF files, link PDF fakebook pages, or type directly. Your existing chord charts work perfectly — 3,000+ song libraries are no problem.

2

Build Your Setlists

Organise songs into playlists for each gig. Drag to reorder on the night. Add pre-show images and intermission slides to each playlist.

3

Connect Your Band

ShowTime runs on your laptop or Raspberry Pi. Band members connect via their phone or tablet browser — no app install, no accounts. You control, they follow.

4

Play the Show

Navigate with keyboard, foot pedal, or touch. The roadie runs the projector from their phone. Everyone stays in sync. Focus on the music.

Screenshots

See It in Action

Real software, real gigs, real chord charts.

Landscape two-column view — full chord chart with lyrics, ring binder spine, and the complete toolbar. Supports portrait, landscape, and page-flip book modes.

Music notation alongside chord charts — MusicXML sheet music displayed via OpenSheetMusicDisplay, side-by-side with the chord chart. Switch between arrangements with one tap: simplified chart, full notation, or both.

Mirror mode — flip the entire display horizontally so a performer reading a reflected screen (music stand mirror, teleprompter) sees everything the right way around.

Media Manager — upload images and videos for Pre-Show, Intermission, Post-Show, or General use. Set display mode (sequential slideshow, random, video loop), transition effects, duration, and Ken Burns zoom. Roadie opens the projection display on any device via the local network.

ShowTime Crew mobile app — crew members scan the QR code and join from any phone. Select your role (Sound, Lighting, Stage Manager, Roadie, Vocalist, General) and receive messages and cues directly from the band leader. No app install — just a browser.

Crew messaging — send instant pre-defined messages to your crew during the show. Colour-coded by urgency: red for critical (foldback issues, FOH problems), yellow for adjustments. Crew members receive alerts on their phone in real time.

Built-in chart editor — font controls, bold/italic/super/subscript, section colour buttons (Intro, Verse, Chorus, Bridge, Outro, Solo), page breaks, chord/lyric size toggles, and a music symbols panel with repeats, D.S., D.C., bar counts, and time signatures.

Song search & band connect — instant search across your entire library with artist names and playlist usage counts. The QR code on the right lets band members connect instantly from any phone or tablet — no app install, just scan and open a browser.

Circle of Fifths popup — interactive reference with tabs for Basic, Improvisation, Major Scale Chords, 2-5-1 and 6-2-5-1 progressions. Note the status bar: Connected — Slave Mode, showing live master/slave sync in action.

Song metadata — store key, BPM, time signature, Alesis SR-18 drum machine pattern, piano setting, genre, and personal notes alongside every song.

Links & media per song — attach YouTube tutorials, Spotify tracks, sheet music PDFs, or any URL. Upload images and videos for projection during the song.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

ShowTime runs a local web server on your main device (the Master — typically a laptop or Raspberry Pi). Other band members open a browser on their phone or tablet and type in your device's IP address. When you change songs, transpose, or turn the page, every connected screen updates instantly over your local WiFi network. No internet required, no accounts, no app installs.

The Master device (where you run ShowTime) can be any Windows, Mac, or Linux machine — or a Raspberry Pi 5 for a compact, silent stage solution. Band members just need any device with a browser: phone, tablet, or laptop. For projection, connect any HDMI display or TV. A small WiFi router dedicated to the band is recommended for reliable sync at a venue.

No. ShowTime runs entirely offline. You just need a local WiFi network for band members to connect to your Master device — even a cheap travel router or mobile hotspot works perfectly. No cloud dependency, no lag, no relying on the venue's WiFi.

You can paste directly from Word, import RTF files, or type and edit in the built-in editor. PDF fakebook pages can be added as chart images. MusicXML files display as full sheet music via OpenSheetMusicDisplay. The editor preserves your chord colours (yellow chords, cyan section headers, etc.) and existing formatting.
